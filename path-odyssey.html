Activity 3 · Imagine futures

Three Odyssey Plans

There isn't one right future — there are many. So instead of betting everything on a single plan, you'll sketch three very different 5-year lives. Seeing them side by side loosens the grip of “the one plan” and shows you what actually pulls at you.

🔒 One at a time — no peeking. Only your first plan is open right now. Finish it (a title and at least three years), and your second plan unlocks — with a twist we'll spring on you then. Finish that one and the third appears. Half the fun is not knowing what's coming.

For each plan you'll add

A six-word title (a headline for the life), a 5-year timeline (a line or two per year), 2–3 questions the plan is asking, and four gauges:

  • Resources — do you have the time, money, and skill?
  • Likability — how much do you like this plan?
  • Confidence — how sure are you that you could pull it off?
  • Coherence — does it fit your Workview & Lifeview?

A sample title & question:

“Creating community, one drink at a time.” — asking: Am I ready for this much risk? Can a bar really build community? Will it pay the bills?

Everything saves automatically in this browser — nothing is uploaded.

When all three are done, share them with 3–5 supportive people. Ask only for “tell me more about…” curiosity — no critique, no advice. Notice which plan makes you lean forward.

Adapted from the Odyssey Plans exercise in Designing Your Life (Burnett & Evans).
