Activity 5 · Build failure immunity

Failure Reframe

You're going to fail sometimes — that's not a bug in the plan, it's how prototypes work. This exercise turns failures from things you carry into things you harvest: sort them, learn from them, and let most of them go.

Why do this?

Designers expect to fail early and often — it's how you find what works. But un-processed failures pile up as shame and second-guessing. Sorting them does two things: it shrinks the ones that don't deserve your energy, and it squeezes a usable lesson out of the ones that do. Do this once or twice a month and failure stops being scary.

How it works

  1. Log a few recent times you messed up — this week, this month, or your all-time hits.
  2. Sort each one into a type (below).
  3. Harvest a lesson from the ones worth learning from, and write the tiny next-time action.

The three types

ScrewupA simple slip. You normally get this right.→ Acknowledge, apologize if needed, move on.
WeaknessA repeat failure from an abiding shortcoming.→ Work around it; avoid the triggers.
Growth opportunityA failure you could fix next time.→ Learn the lesson and improve.

An example

One entry, worked through:

What happened: Called a client, launched straight into my agenda, and she got upset.
Type: Growth opportunity — not a screwup (it was a pattern, not a slip) and not a weakness (I'm normally good on client calls).
Lesson: Always do a human check-in before business.
Next time: Open every call with “What's new since we last talked?”
