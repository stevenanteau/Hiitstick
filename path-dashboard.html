Activity 1 · Start where you are

The Life Dashboard

Before you design anything, it helps to see where you're starting from. This dashboard gives you four gauges — Health, Work, Play, and Love — so you can take an honest reading of your life right now.

Why do this?

You can't tell where to steer until you know where you are. Rating these four areas surfaces your “red lights” — the places running low that deserve design attention. It also helps you separate real, workable problems from “gravity problems” — unactionable facts of life (like “poets don't make much money”) that you can only accept and design around, not solve.

How it works

  • Health — your physical, mental, and spiritual wellbeing.
  • Work — every way you contribute: paid job, caregiving, volunteering, side projects.
  • Play — anything you do purely for joy, with no goal beyond the doing.
  • Love — the flow of connection to and from the people in your life.

Slide each gauge from Empty to Full, jot a sentence or two about what's going on, then answer three quick reflection questions. Nothing is graded — this is a snapshot, not a scorecard.

An example

Someone mid-career might read like this:

Health · ¾
Work · ½
Play · ¼ ⚠
Love · Full

Red light: Play is running low — lots of doing for others, not much doing for joy. That's where they'd design first.
