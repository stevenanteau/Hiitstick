Activity 6 · Get unstuck

Mind Mapping

Stuck usually means “out of ideas.” A mind map fixes that fast: you free-associate around a word until surprising combinations appear — and one of them might be your next thing to try.

How it works

  1. Put a word you care about in the center — something that engages or energizes you.
  2. Add branches: the first words it makes you think of. Don't judge them.
  3. Off each branch, add twigs: quick free-associations. Work fast, bypass your inner censor.
  4. Look at the outer edge, grab three items that jump out, and combine them into a wild new idea.

The goal is quantity and surprise, not a tidy diagram. Weird is good.

Your mind map

Combine into concepts

Pick three words from the outer edge that spark something, and mash them into a possible role, project, or adventure. The stranger the better — you can tame it later.
